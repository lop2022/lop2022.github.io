      Suv apple store

Published on Nov 24, 2022 by Admin.

According to officials, one man was killed and at least 16 others were injured when an SUV crashed into a Massachusetts Apple store on Monday morning.

Around 10:45 a.m. on Monday, police received calls about a car that crashed into storefront windows at the Derby Street Shops in Hingham, about 16 miles southeast of downtown Boston.

Responding officers discovered multiple injured people in and around the Apple store at the outdoor mall, according to Plymouth County District Attorney Timothy Cruz during a news conference on the incident.

According to Cruz, a dark-colored 2019 Toyota 4Runner smashed through the Apple Store's familiar large glass windows at an unknown speed, striking multiple people.

The vehicle drove straight through the store, pinning some customers and employees against the wall. Authorities did not comment on how crowded the store was at the time of the incident, but Cruz stated that "the Apple Store is obviously a very busy place."

"This morning was unthinkable, and people are still trying to process what happened," Cruz said.

Some of those injured were treated by bystanders, according to Hingham Fire Department Chief Steve Murphy, who also spoke at the news conference.




Police identified one male victim as Kevin Bradley, 65, of New Jersey, who was pronounced dead at the scene. According to an NBC News report, Apple identified him as "a professional who was onsite supporting recent construction at the store."

"Our hearts go out to our injured team members and customers, as well as everyone else affected by this terrible incident. We are doing everything possible to assist our team members and customers during this difficult time "Apple stated.

The other 16 victims were transported to local hospitals for treatment.

South Shore Hospital's chief of trauma, Dr. Christopher Burns, said there were "multiple patients with life-threatening and limb-threatening injuries."

South Shore Dr. William Tollefsen said at a press conference that a nearby restaurant was used to help triage patients and get them out of the cold.

According to Cruz, the SUV was driven by a man who has not been identified but is "with police officers." According to officials, the incident is being investigated as a criminal offense.
