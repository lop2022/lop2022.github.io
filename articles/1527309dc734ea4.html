      Massachusetts apple store crash

Published on Nov 24, 2022 by Admin.

An SUV crashed into an Apple store at the Hingham Derby Street Shops shortly before 11 a.m. Monday, killing one person and injuring 19 others.

The 2019 Toyota 4Runner plowed into the store around 10:45 a.m., smashing through the glass and striking multiple people, according to Plymouth County District Attorney Tim Cruz at a news conference.

Sky 5 video showed a large hole in the store's glass front moments after the crash. Several people were on the ground outside the store, being treated by the dozens of first responders on the scene. Cruz stated that a criminal investigation was underway. Authorities did not immediately identify the driver.

"This morning was unthinkable, and people are still trying to process what happened," Cruz said. "This investigation is ongoing and active."

The store opened at 10 a.m. on Monday, and the accident occurred less than an hour later.

Cruz stated that the number of people inside the store at the time was unknown.

"Really... absolutely unthinkable event that occurred at that store when you're just going there to buy a phone or get something fixed or whatever," Cruz said.

The SUV was towed from the store shortly after 3 p.m. Monday.

According to an Apple statement, the victim was a construction worker.




"We are devastated by the shocking events at Apple Derby Street today, as well as the tragic loss of a professional who was onsite supporting recent store construction," Apple said in a written statement. "Our hearts go out to our injured team members and customers, as well as everyone else affected by this terrible incident."

The victim was identified as Kevin Bradley, 65, of New Jersey, by the District Attorney's Office.

According to South Shore Health, 17 patients were being treated for injuries ranging from head trauma to limb injuries.

"We have multiple patients with life-threatening and limb-threatening injuries," said Dr. Christopher Burns, South Shore Health's chief of trauma. "We have patients in the operating room and people who are scheduled to go in."

According to South Shore Health, all patients have been reunited with family members or loved ones.

"We received a hilarious phone call. One minute, it's just another workday. Following that, a car crashes through the building "said John Dinanno, whose daughter was injured in the crash and was taken to South Shore Hospital before being released Monday night. "We're just relieved that she'll be fine. I just thank God she's okay. Just something to be thankful for as Thanksgiving approaches."

"She was hit, and she claimed to have a broken bone and cuts and scrapes all over her body. She's just in a lot of pain "Susan Gamble, whose niece was taken to South Shore Hospital following the crash, agreed. "In fact, she went there last night and forgot her wallet, so she had to go back today, and then this happened."

Authorities said at a press conference on Monday afternoon that two more patients had been transported to hospitals in the Boston area. One was transported to Boston Medical Center, and the other to Brigham and Women's Hospital.
