      Derby st shops

Published on Nov 24, 2022 by Admin.

A vehicle smashed through a storefront at a shopping plaza in Hingham on Monday morning, killing one person and injuring more than a dozen others.

When the vehicle crashed into the Apple store at the Derby Street Shoppes, a technical rescue involving multiple fire departments was required on Derby Street shortly after 10 a.m.

The deceased was later identified as Kevin Bradley, 65, of New Jersey.

After the crash, some victims appeared to be trapped, according to Hanover Fire.

By 11:30 a.m., multiple victims on stretchers were being treated by EMS teams as crews set up a scene, taped off a large section of the plaza.

17 of the crash victims were reportedly taken to South Shore Hospital for treatment, while two others were taken to Boston hospitals.

The driver was also reportedly trapped in the vehicle at one point, though no information on his condition has been released. The driver is believed to be a male in his early 50s and was reportedly speaking with officers at the Hingham Police Department headquarters as of 1 p.m.




According to one witness, he had just left the Apple store and was on his way to the nearby Barnes Noble when the crash occurred. He stated that the vehicle, later identified as an SUV, appeared to crash through the shop's glass facade and ended up on the other end of the building, seriously injuring at least two people at the storefront.

Other witnesses told him the vehicle sped down the parking lot leading up to the Apple store before crashing through the front door. Others claimed that a number of people were pinned inside the store after the SUV crashed in.

"We are devastated by the shocking events at Apple Derby Street today and the tragic loss of a professional who was onsite supporting recent construction at the store," Apple said in a statement.

The statement went on: "Our hearts go out to our injured team members and customers, as well as everyone else affected by this terrible incident. We are doing everything possible to assist our team members and customers during this difficult time."

According to a statement, the Town of Hingham expressed "its deepest condolences and support to those affected by this tragedy." "The Town, like others who have recently suffered loss or injury, will assist those who reach out for help. We are not alone. We are a Town that stands together."

Following the crash, Plymouth District Attorney Timothy J. Cruz stated that a criminal investigation was underway, but no further details could be provided.
