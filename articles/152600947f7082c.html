      Derby street car crash

Published on Nov 24, 2022 by Admin.

Article sharing enables a subscriber to freely share an article with friends and family.

A driver crashed their car through the glass storefront of a Hingham Apple Store during the start of the busy holiday shopping week, killing at least one person and injuring more than a dozen others. "This morning was an unthinkable morning," Plymouth County District Attorney Tim Cruz said at the scene Monday.

A car had crashed through the exterior wall of the Apple Store, according to police and rescue crews who arrived on Derby Street shortly after 10:45 a.m. According to Hingham Fire Chief Steve Murphy, when emergency responders arrived, they discovered at least 16 people had been injured and one person had died.

Cruz stated that a criminal investigation had been launched and that the driver was in custody.

"Right now, family members are being notified or are in the process of being notified, and they have gathered nearby to gather information," Cruz said. "It goes without saying that employees who worked in that store or nearby are visibly shaken."

According to witnesses on the scene, the car appeared to accelerate into the store.

According to Cruz, the driver was injured but not seriously and was able to communicate with first responders. As of Monday morning, the man was in police custody.




"At this point, we are very limited in what we can say," Cruz admitted.

According to preliminary investigations, the dark colored SUV drove through the glass exterior of the Apple Store "at an undetermined rate of speed" before striking multiple people, according to Cruz.

Some of those injured were pinned to the store's inside wall, he added. A technical rescue team was among those who responded.

Officials did not release the driver's name or provide any information about how or why the crash occurred.

"Concerning the driver of the motor vehicle, we are investigating. Concerning the status of the motor vehicle, we are investigating. And, of course, we'll provide whatever information we can "Cruz stated.

Abington, Weymouth, Hull, and Scituate firefighters were also on the scene. According to fire officials, victims were transported to both South Shore Hospital in Weymouth and hospitals in the Boston area.
