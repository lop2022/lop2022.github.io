      Derby st apple store

Published on Nov 24, 2022 by Admin.

Authorities said an SUV crashed through the front window of an Apple store in Massachusetts on Monday, killing one person and injuring 16 others.

Police were investigating but did not say whether the crash was considered accidental. Hingham Police Chief David Jones would only say that an investigation was underway. A 2019 Toyota 4Runner driver was being interviewed.

"This morning was an unthinkable morning," Plymouth District Attorney Timothy Cruz said at a press conference.

The victim has been identified as Kevin Bradley, 65, of New Jersey. Officials said he was pronounced dead at the scene.

Cruz did not say whether the driver would face charges, but he did say that a criminal investigation was underway. The identity of the driver has not been released by authorities.

"This investigation is active and ongoing," he said, adding that the dark-colored SUV smashed through the plate glass window of the store and struck several people.




In a statement, Apple said it was "devastated by the shocking events at Apple Derby Street today and the tragic loss of a professional who was onsite supporting recent store construction."

"Our hearts go out to our injured team members and customers, as well as all those affected by this terrible incident," the company said.

As first responders worked at the scene of the crash shortly before 11 a.m., a large hole gaped in the storefront window. The store, which is located in the Derby Street Shops, was set to open about an hour before the crash.

Hingham Fire Chief Steve Murphy said crews were evaluating the structural stability of the building.
